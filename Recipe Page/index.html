Sambar

Warm, tangy, and laced with aromatic flavors, sambar is the comforting heartbeat of South Indian kitchens. This recipe keeps it simple while staying authentic.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 cup mixed veggies (carrot, drumstick, onion, tomato)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder

For Tempering

  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon hing (asafoetida)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • Optional: pinch of jaggery for balance

Instructions

  1. Cook the Dal Wash and pressure cook toor dal with turmeric until soft. Mash and keep aside.
  2. Prepare the Veggies In a pot, boil the veggies with a pinch of salt until tender.
  3. Make the Sambar Base Add cooked dal, tamarind paste, sambar powder, and salt to the boiled veggies. Simmer for 10–15 minutes until everything blends beautifully.
  4. Temper Like a Pro Heat oil/ghee, add mustard seeds, cumin, hing, curry leaves, and red chilies. Pour this sizzling tempering over the sambar.
  5. Optional Touch Stir in a pinch of jaggery for a rounded flavor profile. Garnish with coriander if you’re feeling fancy!
